# Privacy Policy for Epic Blocks

Effective date: 8 May 2026  
Last updated: 7 June 2026

Warren Row Games ("we", "us", or "our") operates the Epic Blocks mobile game for Android.

This Privacy Policy explains what information Epic Blocks may collect, how that information is used, and the choices available to users and parents or guardians.

Epic Blocks is designed to collect as little personal information as possible. Epic Blocks does not currently include third-party advertising SDKs.

## 1. Information We Collect

Epic Blocks may process limited information depending on the features used.

### a. Gameplay and app data

Epic Blocks may store gameplay-related information, including:

- Game progress
- Scores
- Statistics
- Achievements and achievement progress
- Settings and preferences
- Local save data stored on the user's device

### b. Google Play Games Services data

If Google Play Games Services are available and enabled, or if the user chooses to sign in with Google Play Games Services, Google may provide functionality related to:

- Player sign-in
- Achievements
- Leaderboards
- Saved game or cloud sync features, where available
- Player profile and account identifiers managed by Google
- Public player name or profile information shown in Google Play Games surfaces

This information is handled in accordance with Google's own privacy policies and the user's Google account settings.

Where Epic Blocks opens Google Play Games leaderboard or achievement interfaces, public player names and related profile identity elements may be displayed through Google's systems rather than an Epic Blocks-operated identity service.

### c. Device and technical data

Epic Blocks and the Google Play or Android platform services used with the app may process limited technical data, such as:

- Device type
- Operating system version
- App version
- Crash or diagnostic information
- Information needed to maintain app functionality, security, and compatibility

## 2. How We Use Information

We use information processed by Epic Blocks to:

- Provide gameplay features
- Save progress and preferences
- Display achievements and leaderboard features
- Support optional Google Play Games features
- Maintain app functionality, security, and stability
- Diagnose and fix bugs
- Review support requests or reports related to public player-name visibility where applicable

## 3. Data Storage

### Local storage

Epic Blocks may store gameplay-related information locally on the user's device, including:

- Preferences
- Statistics
- Achievements or achievement progress
- Local scores and gameplay history
- Saved game-related data

### Cloud or third-party platform storage

If Google Play Games features are enabled or used, certain data may be stored or processed by Google through Google Play Games Services.

Cloud save or sync features, where available, may depend on Google Play Games Services and the user's Google account settings. Some gameplay data may remain stored only on the user's device.

Epic Blocks does not currently operate its own dedicated external user account system or independent cloud backend.

## 4. Sharing of Information

We do not sell personal information.

Epic Blocks does not currently include third-party advertising SDKs.

We may share or allow processing of limited data only where necessary to provide app functionality through third-party platform services, such as:

- Google Play Games Services
- Google Play / Android platform services

These providers may process information under their own terms and privacy policies.

Epic Blocks does not currently run its own public username platform for players. If public names are shown through Google Play Games, those names are generally governed by Google's account and platform systems.

## 5. Children's Privacy

Epic Blocks may be played by children, but Epic Blocks is not designed to knowingly collect personal information directly from children.

Epic Blocks does not currently include third-party advertising SDKs and does not operate its own user account, chat, messaging, or public profile system.

Some optional platform features, such as Google Play Games Services, may be managed by Google and may depend on the user's Google account, device settings, parental controls, and Google Play settings.

Parents or guardians can help manage a child's experience by:

- Reviewing device and Google Play account settings
- Managing Google Play Games settings
- Clearing app data through Android device settings
- Uninstalling the app
- Contacting us with privacy-related questions

If you are a parent or guardian and believe that a child has provided personal information to us inappropriately, please contact us at privacy@warrenrow.com and we will review the request.

## 6. Your Choices

Users, parents, or guardians may:

- Choose whether or not to use Google Play Games features, if available
- Clear app data through Android device settings
- Uninstall the app at any time
- Manage Google account and Play Games privacy settings through Google
- Manage Google Play Games profile name and related public identity settings through Google, where supported
- Contact us with privacy-related questions

Please note that removing app data or uninstalling the app may delete locally stored game progress unless it has been backed up through platform services.

## 7. Data Deletion

Epic Blocks does not currently operate its own user account database.

Locally stored Epic Blocks data can be deleted by clearing the app's storage through Android device settings or by uninstalling the app.

If Google Play Games Services are used, some data associated with achievements, leaderboards, saved games, or the user's Google Play Games profile may be managed by Google. Relevant Google account and Play Games settings can be managed through Google.

Privacy-related questions or deletion requests can also be sent to:

privacy@warrenrow.com

## 8. Data Retention

We retain no direct server-side user account database for Epic Blocks at this time.

Locally stored data remains on the user's device until it is removed, app storage is cleared, or the app is uninstalled.

Where Google Play Games or Google Play services are used, retention may be governed by Google's systems and policies.

## 9. Security and Public Player Names

We take reasonable steps to keep Epic Blocks secure and to minimise unnecessary data collection. However, no method of electronic storage or transmission is completely secure.

Epic Blocks does not currently operate its own public player-name system. Where public player names or profile information appear through Google Play Games Services, those details are generally managed through Google's systems and account settings.

If Epic Blocks introduces its own public naming, profile, community, advertising, analytics, or account features in the future, this Privacy Policy will be updated to describe the related data handling, moderation, reporting, and control options.

## 10. Third-Party Services

Epic Blocks may rely on third-party services provided by Google and the Android platform. Their use of data is governed by their own policies.

Relevant links:

- Google Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy
- Google Play Games Services: https://developers.google.com/games/services

## 11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.

If changes materially affect how information is collected, used, or shared, we will take reasonable steps to make the updated policy available to users.
